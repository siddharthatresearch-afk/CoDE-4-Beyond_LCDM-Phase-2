# Matter-Radiation Equality

Matter-radiation equality occurs when the physical energy densities of matter and radiation are equal:

$$
\rho_m(a) = \rho_r(a)
$$

Using standard cosmological scaling relations:

$$
\rho_m(a) \propto a^{-3}
$$

and

$$
\rho_r(a) \propto a^{-4}
$$

the equality epoch occurs at a redshift of:

$$
1+z_{eq} = \frac{\Omega_m}{\Omega_r}
$$

where:
- $\Omega_m$ is the total matter density parameter (baryons + cold dark matter),
- and $\Omega_r$ is the total radiation density parameter (photons + relativistic neutrinos).

# Importance of Equality

The equality epoch acts as a critical transition in cosmological evolution.

Before equality:
- radiation dominates the expansion dynamics,
- perturbation growth is strongly suppressed,
- and relativistic effects dominate structure evolution.

After equality:
- matter domination begins,
- gravitational instability becomes efficient,
- and large-scale structure formation accelerates.

Because of this, even small changes to the equality epoch can significantly affect:
- structure growth,
- matter power spectra,
- CMB consistency,
- and clustering evolution.
