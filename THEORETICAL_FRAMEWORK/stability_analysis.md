# Stability Analysis

## Overview

A viable cosmological framework must remain mathematically and dynamically stable across the full range of cosmological evolution.

Because CoDE-4 introduces a perturbative late-time deformation of the cosmological expansion sector, an important requirement of the framework is that the deformation:
- remains bounded,
- avoids divergent evolution,
- preserves early-universe consistency,
- and does not generate pathological perturbative behavior.

The current CoDE-4 framework was intentionally constructed as a weak phenomenological deformation of standard cosmological expansion in order to preserve these stability properties.

---

# Bounded Deformation Structure

The CoDE-4 framework modifies the effective vacuum sector through the bounded correction function:

$$
C(z) = 1 + \epsilon \left(\frac{z}{(1+z)^2}\right)\left(1-\frac{0.15}{1+z}\right)
$$

The deformation was designed such that:
- the correction remains finite for all finite redshift,
- the modification vanishes asymptotically at high redshift,
- and no singular expansion behavior emerges.

The correction therefore acts as:
- a perturbative late-time deformation,
rather than:
- a strongly divergent cosmological modification.

---

# Asymptotic Recovery

At high redshift:

$$
z \to \infty
$$

the framework satisfies:

$$
C(z) \to 1
$$

This asymptotic suppression ensures:
- recovery of near-standard radiation-era evolution,
- preservation of recombination physics,
- stable early-universe expansion,
- and near-standard perturbation growth.

The asymptotic recovery mechanism is one of the primary stability properties of the framework.

---

# Expansion Stability

The modified expansion equation is written as:

$$
H^2(z) = H_0^2 \left[ \Omega_m(1+z)^3 + \Omega_r(1+z)^4 + \Omega_\Lambda C(z) \right]
$$

Because:
- the matter sector remains positive,
- the radiation sector remains positive,
- and the deformation remains bounded,

the total expansion evolution remains finite throughout the tested cosmological range.

Current numerical evolution demonstrates:
- stable expansion behavior,
- smooth late-time evolution,
- and no divergent expansion regime.

# Early-Universe Stability

The CoDE-4 numerical engine explicitly evaluates:
- Big Bang Nucleosynthesis consistency,
- radiation-era expansion,
- CMB damping-tail behavior,
- and recombination-era evolution.

Current numerical results produce:
- approximately zero-percent BBN deviation,
- sub-percent damping-tail deviations,
- and preserved high-redshift expansion structure.

These results indicate:
- stable early-universe evolution,
- preserved radiation domination,
- and asymptotic recovery of standard cosmology.

# Perturbative Stability

Linear matter perturbations are evolved numerically using the modified background expansion history with respect to the scale factor domain $a$ (where $' \equiv d/da$):

$$
\delta'' + \left( \frac{3}{a} + \frac{H'}{H} \right)\delta' - \frac{3 H_0^2 \Omega_m}{2 a^5 H^2}\delta = 0
$$

The perturbation evolution remains:
- finite,
- smooth,
- bounded,
- and numerically stable across the tested redshift range.

The framework does not currently exhibit:
- runaway growth,
- divergent perturbation amplitudes,
- or unstable clustering evolution.

The growth sector instead behaves as:
- a weak perturbative deformation of standard cosmological evolution.

---

# Acoustic Stability

The acoustic sector of CoDE-4 additionally remains highly stable.

Current numerical reconstruction demonstrates:
- preserved sound-horizon evolution,
- stable recombination geometry,
- and strong first acoustic peak consistency.

The framework currently reproduces:

$$
l_1 \approx 220.72
$$

with extremely small deviation relative to standard observational constraints.

This indicates that the late-time deformation does not significantly disrupt early-universe plasma oscillations.

# ISW Stability

The Integrated Sachs-Wolfe sector provides an important dynamical consistency test.

The CoDE-4 numerical engine evaluates normalized linear gravitational potential evolution on sub-horizon scales through:

$$
\Phi(a) = \frac{\delta(a)}{a}
$$

Current numerical evolution demonstrates:
- stable gravitational potential decay,
- negative $d\Phi/d\ln(a)$ behavior,
- and standard dark-energy-dominated ISW evolution.

This suggests that the framework preserves stable late-time perturbative behavior.

---

# Equality Stability

The framework additionally preserves near-standard matter-radiation equality evolution.

Current numerical evolution produces:

$$
z_{eq} \approx 3499
$$

which remains within the standard expected cosmological range.

This preservation is important because unstable equality evolution would strongly affect:
- structure formation,
- matter power spectra,
- and recombination consistency.

---

# Residual Tension Sector

The primary remaining phenomenological tension currently arises in:
- weak-lensing clustering amplitudes,
- matter power-spectrum turnover behavior,
- and the $S_8$ sector.

However, these tensions represent:
- observational consistency limitations,
rather than:
- mathematical or dynamical instabilities.

The framework remains numerically stable throughout tested cosmological evolution.

Overall, the current CoDE-4 framework exhibits:
- bounded deformation behavior,
- asymptotic recovery of standard cosmology,
- stable perturbative evolution,
- preserved recombination structure,

- and numerically consistent early-universe behavior.

The framework therefore currently behaves as:
- a stable phenomenological modified-expansion cosmology framework,
