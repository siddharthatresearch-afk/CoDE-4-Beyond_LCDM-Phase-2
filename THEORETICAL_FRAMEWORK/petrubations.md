# Cosmological Perturbation Framework

## Overview

Cosmological perturbation theory provides the connection between background cosmological expansion and observable structure formation.

While the CoDE-4 framework modifies the late-time expansion history through a bounded perturbative vacuum deformation, observable cosmology is also sensitive to how matter perturbations evolve within the modified background geometry.

For this reason, perturbation evolution forms an important consistency sector of the framework.

---

# Background Expansion and Perturbations

The CoDE-4 framework modifies the cosmological expansion history through the effective deformation function:

$$
C(z) = 1 + \epsilon \left( \frac{z}{(1+z)^2} \right) \left( 1 - \frac{0.15}{1+z} \right)
$$

leading to the modified expansion equation:

$$
H^2(z) = H_0^2 \left[ \Omega_m(1+z)^3 + \Omega_r(1+z)^4 + \Omega_\Lambda C(z) \right]
$$

The perturbation sector itself is not fundamentally altered at the equation level. Instead, CoDE-4 modifies the background expansion function $H(a)$ that governs perturbation evolution.

This means the framework behaves as:
- a modified-background perturbation model,
rather than a fundamentally modified gravity theory.

# Linear Matter Perturbations

Linear matter perturbations evolve according to the standard growth equation written with respect to the scale factor $a$ (where $a = \frac{1}{1+z}$):

$$
\delta'' + \left( \frac{3}{a} + \frac{H'}{H} \right)\delta' - \frac{3 H_0^2 \Omega_m}{2 a^5 H^2}\delta = 0
$$

where:
- $\delta$ is the linear matter density contrast ($\delta\rho_m/\rho_m$),
- $a$ is the cosmological scale factor,
- and primes denote derivatives with respect to $a$ ($d/da$).

The modified CoDE-4 background expansion changes:
- perturbation friction,
- growth suppression,
- and clustering evolution.

Because CoDE-4 modifies the late-time expansion sector, the framework alters:
