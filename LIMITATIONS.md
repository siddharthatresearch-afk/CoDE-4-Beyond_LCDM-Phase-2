# LIMITATIONS

# Scientific Scope and Current Limitations of CoDE-4

The CoDE-4 framework is currently intended as an exploratory phenomenological cosmology project designed to investigate whether mild late-time modifications to expansion history can preserve approximate cross-epoch observational consistency while reducing the inferred Hubble tension.

The framework should not presently be interpreted as:
- a complete replacement for ΛCDM,
- a fundamental theory of gravity,
- or a statistically validated cosmological model.

Several important theoretical and numerical limitations currently remain.

---

# 1. Phenomenological Nature of the Model

The CoDE-4 modification function:

$$
C(z)=1+\epsilon\left(\frac{z}{(1+z)^2}\right)\left(1-\frac{0.15}{1+z}\right)
$$

is phenomenological.

It is not currently derived from:
- a covariant action,
- modified Einstein field equations,
- scalar-field dynamics,
- effective field theory,
- quantum gravity,
- or any established particle-physics framework.

The current implementation is therefore best interpreted as a controlled expansion-history parametrization rather than a fully fundamental cosmological theory.

---

# 2. No Einstein–Boltzmann Evolution

The framework does not currently implement:
- photon hierarchy evolution,
- neutrino transport,
- recombination microphysics,
- full perturbation transfer functions,
- or Einstein–Boltzmann evolution.

As a result:
- the CMB spectra are approximate phenomenological reconstructions,
- not precision CAMB/CLASS-level predictions,
- and should not be interpreted as full likelihood-compatible CMB outputs.

---

# 3. No MCMC or Likelihood Analysis

The framework currently does not include:
- Markov Chain Monte Carlo parameter estimation,
- Bayesian likelihood fitting,
- covariance analysis,
- survey likelihood pipelines,
- or posterior parameter inference.

Consequently:
- the reported observational agreements are approximate consistency studies,
- not statistically optimized best-fit cosmological constraints.

---

# 4. Approximate Matter Power Spectrum Treatment

The matter power spectrum calculations are simplified and primarily intended for qualitative large-scale consistency analysis.

The framework does not currently include:
- nonlinear clustering corrections,
- halo-model evolution,
- baryonic feedback,
- or full transfer-function reconstruction.

The observed turnover-scale deviation therefore remains approximate.

---

# 5. Weak-Lensing Tension Remains

Although the framework substantially shifts the inferred Hubble expansion rate, the predicted:

$$
S_8
$$

value remains relatively close to Planck-era clustering amplitudes.

As a result:
- weak-lensing tension is only partially alleviated,
- and the framework does not currently provide a complete simultaneous resolution of all late-time cosmological tensions.

---

# 6. Sound Horizon Compression

The framework produces a reduced sound horizon relative to standard ΛCDM expectations.

This contributes directly to the inferred increase in:

$$
H_0
$$

and may represent an implicit geometric tuning mechanism within the current phenomenological implementation.

Further investigation is required to determine whether this behavior can emerge naturally within a more fundamental physical framework.

---

# 7. Approximate Early-Galaxy Interpretation

The framework explores whether modest enhancement of high-redshift structure growth may ease timing constraints associated with early JWST-era galaxy observations.

However:
- no direct galaxy-formation simulation,
- hydrodynamic modeling,
- or baryonic evolution analysis

has yet been implemented.

Consequently:
- any connection to JWST observations should currently be interpreted as phenomenological and exploratory rather than predictive.

---

# 8. Numerical Simplifications

Several numerical sectors remain simplified, including:
- growth evolution,
- distance integrations,
- and perturbation handling.

The current implementation prioritizes:
- conceptual transparency,
- computational stability,
- and cross-epoch consistency testing

rather than precision survey-level forecasting.

---

# 9. No Covariant Consistency Proof

The framework has not yet demonstrated:
- covariant conservation consistency,
- perturbative stability at all epochs,
- ghost-free behavior,
- or compatibility with a fully self-consistent relativistic field formulation.

The current implementation should therefore not yet be interpreted as a complete modified-gravity theory.

---

# 10. Expansion-Tension Reduction Is Model-Dependent

The reduction in inferred Hubble tension emerges from the specific recursive acoustic-scale calibration mechanism used by the framework.

This behavior is therefore model-dependent and should not currently be interpreted as definitive evidence for a physical resolution of cosmological tensions.

---

# Current Scientific Interpretation

At its current stage, CoDE-4 is best interpreted as:

- a phenomenological exploratory cosmology framework,
- a computational cross-epoch consistency investigation,
- and a numerical experiment in coordinated expansion–growth evolution.

The framework is intended to explore whether mild late-time departures from ΛCDM expansion history can preserve broad geometric consistency across multiple cosmological epochs simultaneously.

Further development would require:
- full Einstein–Boltzmann evolution,
- likelihood analysis,
- covariance fitting,
- perturbative consistency analysis,
- and direct survey-level statistical comparison.
